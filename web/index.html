Every burn gives you a shot in the current 17-minute round. The token burn is real. The football animation is theatre. After the timer ends, the first buy, sell, or burn locks a future block seed; a later touch pays the onchain winner.

Trade tax

How the opening split works

For the first 77 minutes, every buy and sell pays 6.0% total: 2.0% API, 1.0% last-buyer pot, 1.0% official LP rewards, and 2.0% penalty draw. After 77 minutes, only the API piece can change, and it can never be higher than 0.8%.

Curve exit

What sell-to-curve means

Before the 17 ETH fill, curve sells return 777X to the opening inventory so the curve can keep functioning. After the fill, the opening buy rail is closed forever, so sell-to-curve burns the returned 777X instead.

Fuse logic

How the crash tax works

The fuse watches the recent high. For the first 6 hours it resets every 30 minutes. After that it switches to 3-hour windows. If price falls below 90%, 80%, or 40% of the live window high, extra sell tax appears. Official LP wallets receive 20.0% of that extra fuse tax.

Round logic

Why the round stays fixed

Keeping the penalty round fixed at 17 minutes is the cleanest path. Users can burn at any time, the math is readable, and payout uses a future block seed after the timer expires. Random windows sound fun, but they are harder to audit and harder to explain.
