How To Play

Buy on Flap. Burn to shoot. Win from the penalty pot.

Oracle777 penalty mode is intentionally simple: Flap launches the token, trade tax feeds the BNB vault, and only users who voluntarily send 777X to the dead address enter the 17-minute Chainlink VRF penalty round. The game is risky. Nothing is guaranteed.

Rules

Plain Version

Flap penalty mode
  1. Buy 777X through Flap. Flap handles the launch rail, trading, and tax-token path. Oracle777 does not need its own bonding curve in this mode.
  2. Trade tax feeds the vault. The target design is 3% buy tax and 3% sell tax. Flap tax inflow goes into the Oracle777 penalty vault.
  3. Dev/API share is temporary. During the first 77 minutes, one third of vault inflow goes to the dev/API wallet. After that, the dev share ends automatically and new vault inflow remains in the penalty pot.
  4. Dead-burn 777X to shoot. Participation is voluntary. Pressing shoot transfers your selected 777X to the dead address and records your weighted shot in the active round.
  5. Bigger burns help, but not linearly. Shot weight uses the square root of the burned amount. A larger burn has better odds, but it does not get a straight-line advantage over smaller wallets.
  6. Returning shooters get a small transparent boost. The vault can add a capped boost based on how long the wallet has been an active shooter. This is not hidden AI scoring and not an offchain profile.
  7. Each settled round pays 30% of the available pot. When a 17-minute round ends, the next onchain touch requests Chainlink VRF. The VRF callback pays the winner automatically for normal wallets.
  8. No last-buyer, no fuse, no LP reward in this mode. Those older arcade modules are hidden on the site. The Flap version keeps the core game as penalty only so the rules stay readable.
