How To Play

Buy 777X. Burn to shoot. Hold the last buyer spot.

Oracle777 is a football arcade built around one simple loop: trades feed ETH pots, burns cut supply, and active players chase outsized rewards. The contract rules are public. The game is risky. Nothing is guaranteed.

Rules

Plain Version

Onchain arcade
  1. Buy on the opening curve. The first buy lane runs until the curve reaches 17 ETH gross buy-in. After that, this opening buy lane closes forever.
  2. Trades fund the game pots. During the first 77 minutes, every buy and sell pays 6.0% total: 2.0% API, 1.0% last-buyer pot, 1.0% official LP rewards, and 2.0% penalty draw.
  3. Only the API rail can change later. After the first 77 minutes, the API rail can only move between 0.0% and 0.8%. The last-buyer, LP, and penalty rails stay fixed by contract.
  4. Burn 777X to enter the penalty draw. Every burn creates an entry in the current 17-minute round. Bigger burns get more weight, but smaller wallets still have a real shot because the weight curve is not linear. When the timer ends, the first onchain touch locks a future block seed; a later touch pays the winner.
  5. LP holders get a clear boost. If your wallet holds an official 777X LP position, the contract can add a small boost to your penalty draw weight and share LP rewards with you. No LP means no LP reward; rewards wait in the LP contract until official LP exists.
  6. The last buyer can win ETH. If you are the latest qualifying buyer and nobody buys again for 45 minutes, the next buy, sell, or burn triggers the ETH payout to you.
  7. Sharp sell drops trigger the fuse. If the price falls under 90%, 80%, or 40% of the recent high, extra sell fees appear: 5%, 10%, or 15%. That extra fuse fee is split 80% to the penalty draw and 20% to official LP rewards.
  8. After the curve is full, sell-back burns tokens. Before 17 ETH is filled, tokens sold back to the curve return to opening inventory. After the fill, the buy rail is closed, so those returned 777X are burned instead. The token cannot be freely minted again.
